This website documents my work term experience as a backend developer working on a digital wallet platform in the FinTech space. It outlines the responsibilities I held, the skills I developed, and the growth I experienced during my first professional work term.

Information About the Employer

ShamadhanPay is a digital payment platform operated by Samadhan Services Limited, a subsidiary of Grameen Telecom. The organization is a licensed Payment Service Provider regulated by Bangladesh Bank and is based in Dhaka, Bangladesh.

The platform provides an e-wallet system that supports peer to peer transfers, bill and utility payments, merchant payments, and digital user onboarding. From a technical perspective, the company operates within the FinTech domain, requiring secure backend systems, reliable transaction processing, and integration with multiple external financial institutions.

Goals

Backend API Development for FinTech Systems

I focused on understanding how backend APIs support real transaction processing across multiple external banking partners. Through reviewing existing services and contributing to backend improvements, I learned how transaction flows are validated, processed, and protected in a financial system.

Debugging and Testing in SIT and UAT

A major part of my role involved investigating transaction failures in SIT and UAT environments. I analyzed logs, traced issues through backend services, and worked with QA and DevOps to resolve problems. This helped me develop a more structured and confident debugging approach.

Communication and Delivery in a Remote Team

Working in a fully remote environment, I focused on clear communication, task organization, and consistent delivery. I provided regular updates, tracked work using Jira, and contributed to documentation to support collaboration within the team.

Job Description

During my work term, I worked on the backend of a digital wallet platform, supporting transaction flows and investigating issues across different testing environments. Much of my work involved reading existing code, debugging failures, and making small but meaningful improvements to backend services.

I primarily worked with Python and SQL, used Git for version control, and relied on Jira to manage tasks and collaborate with the team. Being part of a growing platform used in Bangladesh was especially motivating, as it showed how backend work can contribute to real world impact.

Conclusion

This was my first work term, and I began the experience feeling nervous about contributing to real production systems. Over time, I gained confidence through hands on backend work, debugging, and collaboration within a remote engineering team. By the end of the term, I felt more prepared and excited to continue developing my skills as a software developer.
