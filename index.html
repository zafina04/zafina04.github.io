Information About the Employer

ShamadhanPay is a digital payment platform operated by Samadhan Services Limited, a subsidiary of Grameen Telecom. It is based in Dhaka, Bangladesh. The organization is a licensed Payment Service Provider regulated by Bangladesh Bank.

ShamadhanPay delivers an e-wallet based system that supports core digital financial services such as peer-to-peer transfers, bill and utility payments, merchant payments, and digital user onboarding through e-KYC processes.

From a technical perspective, the platform operates within the FinTech domain, requiring secure backend systems, reliable transaction processing, and integration with multiple external financial institutions. Goals

Backend API Development for FinTech Systems

Throughout my work term, I focused on understanding how backend APIs support real money movement across multiple external banking partners. I reviewed existing service architecture, learned common backend patterns used in the system, and contributed to improving backend functionality tied to transaction processing. By the end of the term, I could clearly explain the full transaction lifecycle, identify common failure points, and understand how backend services enforce validation, consistency, and reliability in a financial system.

Debugging and Testing in SIT and UAT

A major part of my role involved investigating transaction failures in SIT and UAT environments. I analyzed logs, traced errors through backend services, and worked closely with QA and DevOps to determine root causes. Over time, I became more independent in resolving issues and more intentional about writing unit tests to prevent regressions. While I improved significantly, I also learned that effective debugging depends on both system familiarity and a structured approach, which I continue to refine.

Communication and Delivery in a Remote Team

Working in a fully remote team, I focused on maintaining clear communication and staying organized in a fast paced delivery cycle. I tracked tasks carefully, provided regular progress updates, and actively participated in team discussions. I also contributed to technical documentation and ensured my written communication was clear and easy to follow. These habits helped me meet deadlines reliably, collaborate effectively with teammates, and build trust within the team.

Job Description

During my work term, I worked on the backend of a digital wallet platform, where I supported transaction flows and investigated issues across different testing environments. A lot of my time was spent reading existing code, debugging failures, and making small but meaningful improvements to backend services. I worked mainly with Python and SQL, used Git for version control, and relied on Jira to track tasks and coordinate work with the rest of the team.

What I enjoyed most was being part of a product that is still growing and actively used in Bangladesh. Knowing that the platform plays a role in improving digital payments and connectivity made the work feel meaningful, even when the tasks were technical or detail heavy. It was rewarding to see how small backend changes could directly affect how the system behaves in real use.

The role pushed me to develop strong problem solving skills and to communicate clearly in a remote team setting. While my coursework helped with the basics, most of what I learned came from hands on experience, especially debugging real issues, understanding complex workflows, and learning how professional teams collaborate. This work term gave me a clearer picture of what working as a software developer looks like beyond the classroom.

Conclusion

This was my first work term, and going into it I was nervous about working on real production systems for the first time. Over the course of the term, I gained hands on experience with backend development, debugging, and working within a remote engineering team, which helped build my confidence. By the end of the term, I felt more comfortable contributing to real projects and excited about continuing to grow as a software developer.
